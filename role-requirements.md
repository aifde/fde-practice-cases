# OpenAI and Amazon FDE Role Requirements

English | [中文](role-requirements.zh.md) | [日本語](role-requirements.ja.md)

This framework translates current public Forward Deployed Engineer responsibilities at OpenAI and Amazon into eight capabilities that can be observed and practiced through the cases in this repository. It is not an official curriculum, hiring rubric, or endorsement from either company. Job postings change, so applicants should always recheck the live role.

## Capability framework

### R1. Embedded discovery and trusted partnership

Work directly with customer engineers, domain experts, operators, and sponsors. Understand the real workflow, turn business needs into technical requirements, and communicate clearly across technical and executive audiences.

### R2. Scoping and end-to-end delivery ownership

Define success criteria, scope, milestones, dependencies, and acceptance conditions. Own delivery from discovery and prototype through production rollout, while making explicit tradeoffs among speed, scope, quality, and risk.

### R3. Hands-on implementation and systems integration

Write production-grade software, design architectures, connect APIs, data pipelines, databases, cloud services, tools, and existing customer infrastructure. An FDE is expected to build and debug, not only advise.

### R4. AI workflow and human-control design

Understand how model behavior affects the product experience. Define where agents may act, where people review, how context passes between systems, and how refusals, escalation, and trust signals work.

### R5. Evaluation and production quality

Create representative evaluations, quality thresholds, and feedback loops. Monitor production behavior, investigate failures and drift, and use real usage plus human judgment to improve the system.

### R6. Security, governance, reliability, and cost

Design for permissions, data boundaries, auditability, privacy, compliance, reliability, performance, and cost from the start. Production readiness is part of delivery, not a final review.

### R7. Adoption and measurable business impact

Put working software into the actual workflow, support onboarding and change management, and measure adoption and operational outcomes. Shipping alone is not the finish line.

### R8. Reusable patterns and field feedback

Turn deployment lessons into tools, templates, reference architectures, playbooks, and product feedback. The goal is to make later deployments faster and to improve the underlying product or platform.

## What this library does not map

The cases do not attempt to prove years of experience, degrees, location eligibility, travel availability, citizenship, or security-clearance requirements. Those are candidate or role-specific conditions, not properties of a delivery case.
